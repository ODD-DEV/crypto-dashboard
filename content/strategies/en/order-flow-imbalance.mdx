## How It Works

Most retail traders watch price and lagging indicators. This strategy goes deeper — it analyzes the **taker buy/sell volume ratio** to understand *who* is driving the market. When aggressive buyers (takers lifting the ask) significantly outweigh aggressive sellers (takers hitting the bid), it signals institutional accumulation before a price move.

### Taker Volume: The Leading Indicator

Every trade in a crypto order book has a maker and a taker. The taker is the aggressive party — they cross the spread to execute immediately:

- **Taker buy volume**: Aggressive buyers lifting ask orders — bullish pressure
- **Taker sell volume**: Aggressive sellers hitting bid orders — bearish pressure
- **Taker buy ratio**: `taker_buy_volume / total_volume` — above 0.5 means more aggressive buying

When the taker buy ratio reaches extreme levels (above 0.6 or below 0.4), it indicates a strong directional imbalance that often precedes a price move.

### Why Order Flow Leads Price

Price is a *lagging* indicator — it only moves after order flow has shifted. Large institutional players often accumulate positions gradually to avoid moving the price. During this accumulation phase:

1. Taker buy volume quietly rises relative to sell volume
2. Price may appear flat or even slightly declining (as the institution absorbs sellers)
3. Once accumulation is complete, the buying pressure overwhelms available supply
4. Price breaks out sharply

By monitoring the taker ratio, the strategy detects this accumulation phase *before* the price breakout.

### Signal Construction

The strategy builds a composite order flow signal:

1. **Short-term ratio** (5-period): Captures immediate flow shifts
2. **Medium-term ratio** (20-period): Confirms the trend in flow
3. **Divergence detection**: When short-term flow is bullish but price is flat/down, accumulation is likely in progress
4. **Z-score normalization**: The raw ratio is converted to a z-score against the trailing 100-period distribution to identify statistically significant imbalances

A signal triggers when the z-score exceeds a threshold (typically 1.5-2.0 standard deviations).

### Risk Management

- **Position sizing**: Based on the magnitude of the z-score — stronger signals get larger positions
- **Stop loss**: Below the recent swing low for longs, above swing high for shorts
- **Trailing stop**: Activated when the trade reaches 1.5x the initial risk
- **Time exit**: If the expected move doesn't materialize within 24 hours, the position is closed

## Performance Characteristics

- **Moderate win rate** (~58%): Not every flow imbalance leads to a price move
- **High trade frequency**: Flow imbalances are detected more frequently than other signal types
- **Best in trending markets**: When institutions are actively positioning, the strategy thrives
- **Weakest in ranging markets**: Low-conviction flow in ranges produces noisy signals

## Known Limitations

- **Exchange-specific data**: Taker volume data varies between exchanges. The strategy uses Binance data, which may not capture activity on other venues
- **Spoofing risk**: Sophisticated traders can manipulate the taker ratio through rapid order placement and cancellation
- **Latency sensitivity**: By the time the signal is confirmed across multiple periods, some of the move may have already occurred

## Walk-Forward Validation Results

The strategy performed consistently across all 4 out-of-sample quarters, with particularly strong results during trending market phases. The edge is most reliable on high-volume pairs where taker data is most representative of true market intent.
