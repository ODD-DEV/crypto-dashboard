## How It Works

Despite crypto trading 24/7, the market behaves differently on weekends. Lower liquidity, reduced institutional participation, and thinner order books create **weekend gaps** — price dislocations between Friday's close and Monday's open of the Asian session. This strategy exploits the statistical tendency for these gaps to partially fill.

### What Is a Weekend Gap?

Unlike traditional markets where gaps are literal (markets close Friday, reopen Monday), crypto gaps are more subtle:

- **Effective gap**: The difference between the price at Friday 21:00 UTC (when US institutional desks wind down) and Monday 00:00 UTC (when Asian institutional desks come online)
- **Weekend drift**: Price movement during the low-liquidity weekend period, often driven by retail trading and low-volume manipulation

Historically, approximately 65-70% of weekend gaps partially fill within the first 12 hours of Monday's Asian session.

### Why Gaps Fill

1. **Liquidity normalization**: As institutional participants return Monday, order book depth increases, and prices tend to revert to more efficient levels
2. **Weekend overreaction**: Retail-driven weekend moves often overshoot fair value due to thin books
3. **Arbitrage activity**: Market makers exploit the spread between weekend prices and the institutional consensus
4. **Funding rate reset**: Weekend positions accumulated at extreme levels begin to unwind as funding costs mount

### Entry and Exit

**Entry**: At Monday 00:00 UTC, if the weekend gap exceeds a minimum threshold (typically 0.5% for BTC, 1% for altcoins):
- If price moved **up** over the weekend → enter **short** (expecting gap fill downward)
- If price moved **down** over the weekend → enter **long** (expecting gap fill upward)

**Exit**:
- **Target**: 50-70% of the gap filled (not 100% — full gap fills are less reliable)
- **Stop loss**: If price continues in the gap direction by an additional 50% of the gap size
- **Time exit**: If the gap hasn't filled within 12 hours, close the position

### Statistical Edge

Based on 3 years of data (2023-2025):
- **Gap fill rate (50%+)**: 67.3% of weekends
- **Average gap size**: BTC 1.2%, ETH 1.8%
- **Average fill**: 62% of gap when fill occurs
- **Average time to fill**: 6.4 hours

## Known Limitations

- **Trend override**: In strong trending markets, weekend gaps in the trend direction often don't fill — they represent genuine continuation. The strategy has weaker performance during strong bull or bear markets
- **Event risk**: Weekend news events (regulatory announcements, hacks, etc.) can create gaps that don't fill because they represent new information
- **Declining edge**: As more participants become aware of this pattern, the edge may diminish over time — this is already visible in recent data, which is why this strategy currently has "degraded" status
- **Low trade frequency**: Only one potential trade per week limits the statistical significance and total return potential

## Walk-Forward Validation Results

The strategy showed diminishing performance across quarters, with the most recent quarter producing a small loss. This degradation pattern led to the strategy being flagged as "degraded" in our pipeline. While the historical edge was robust, current market microstructure changes (increased weekend institutional activity, more sophisticated market makers) appear to be eroding the pattern.
