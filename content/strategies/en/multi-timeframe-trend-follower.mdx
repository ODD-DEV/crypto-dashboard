## How It Works

Most trend-following strategies operate on a single timeframe and suffer from two problems: too many false signals on short timeframes, and too-late entries on long timeframes. This strategy solves both by requiring **trend agreement across three timeframes** — 5-minute, 1-hour, and 4-hour — before entering a trade.

### The Three-Timeframe Filter

Each timeframe answers a different question:

**4-hour (Strategic)**: "What is the dominant trend?"
- Uses a 50-period EMA slope and ADX reading
- Only takes trades in the direction of this trend
- Acts as the ultimate filter — if the 4H trend is down, no long trades are taken regardless of lower timeframe signals

**1-hour (Tactical)**: "Is the trend currently active?"
- Uses MACD crossover and histogram direction
- Confirms that the strategic trend is being expressed in current price action
- Filters out periods where the 4H trend exists but price is in a consolidation or pullback

**5-minute (Execution)**: "Where do I enter?"
- Uses a fast EMA crossover (8/21 EMA)
- Provides precise entry timing within the confirmed trend
- Only triggers when aligned with both higher timeframes

### Why Triple Confirmation Works

The power of this approach is statistical: each timeframe filter independently rejects ~50% of potential signals. Three filters in series means roughly 1 in 8 potential signals survive — these surviving signals have a significantly higher probability of being genuine trend moves rather than noise.

Consider a scenario:
1. 4H shows uptrend → eliminates downside noise
2. 1H confirms active momentum → eliminates consolidation traps
3. 5M gives entry signal → provides timing precision

A signal that passes all three filters represents a rare alignment where the trend is strong, active, and accelerating.

### Entry Details

When all three timeframes align:
1. Enter at the close of the 5-minute candle that triggers the alignment
2. Position size: Standard allocation (no scaling based on signal strength — the triple filter is the quality control)
3. Initial stop: Below the most recent 5-minute swing low (for longs)

### Exit: Trailing Stop

The strategy uses a **trailing stop** rather than a fixed take-profit:

- **Initial trailing distance**: 2x ATR on the 5-minute chart
- **Trail activation**: After the trade reaches 1x ATR in profit
- **Trail tightening**: As profit increases, the trailing distance gradually narrows from 2x ATR to 1.5x ATR
- **Timeframe break exit**: If the 1-hour timeframe loses its trend signal (MACD crosses against the position), the trailing stop tightens to 0.5x ATR regardless of profit level

This approach lets winning trades run while cutting losses quickly when the multi-timeframe alignment breaks down.

### Timeout Mechanism

If a trade hasn't hit either the trailing stop or achieved minimum profit within 48 candles (4 hours on the 5-minute chart), it's closed at market price. This prevents capital from being tied up in trades where the expected move never materialized.

## Performance Profile

- **Moderate win rate** (~55%): The strict filter means fewer but higher-quality entries
- **Favorable risk/reward**: Average winner is typically 1.8-2.2x the average loser
- **Trend-dependent**: Performs exceptionally well in trending markets, underperforms in choppy ranges
- **Sector rotation**: Different coins trend at different times — the strategy naturally rotates to whichever coins are trending most strongly

## Known Limitations

- **Late entries**: By the time all three timeframes align, a portion of the trend move has already occurred. The strategy trades quality over timing
- **Whipsaw in transitions**: When the market transitions from trending to ranging (or vice versa), the strategy may generate a cluster of losing trades
- **Higher timeframe lag**: The 4-hour trend filter updates slowly, which means the strategy can remain in "no trade" mode for extended periods during market transitions
- **Gap risk**: Strong overnight moves can blow past the trailing stop, resulting in larger-than-expected losses

## Walk-Forward Validation Results

The strategy demonstrated positive returns in 3 out of 4 out-of-sample quarters, with one losing quarter occurring during a period of extended sideways consolidation. The edge is strongest during sustained directional moves and weakest during regime transitions.
