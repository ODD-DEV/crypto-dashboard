## Discovery

Order flow analysis. It comes from the high-frequency trading (HFT) world, but the core idea is intuitive: order flow moves before price does. When institutions build large positions, the price might not move yet, but the taker (aggressive order) ratio shows hints.

Found an implementation of this concept in an open-source trading library. "Can a retail trader actually use this?" I wondered. Binance provides taker buy/sell volume data via API, so theoretically, yes. Let's find out.

## The Concept

1. **Track taker buy ratio**: `taker_buy_volume / total_volume` (above 0.5 = aggressive buying dominates)
2. **Short-term (5-period) vs medium-term (20-period)**: Short-term spike + medium-term rise = accumulation confirmed
3. **Z-score normalization**: How extreme is the current ratio compared to the last 100 periods?
4. **Divergence detection**: Bullish flow + flat/declining price → accumulation in progress
5. **Entry**: Z-score exceeds 1.5-2.0 standard deviations

## Backtest Setup

- **Period**: Aug 2025 — Feb 2026 (7 months)
- **Coins**: BTC, ETH, SOL, NEAR, APT
- **Timeframe**: 5-minute candles
- **Fees**: 0.05% per side

## Results

156 trades, **cumulative return of 112.8%**. Monthly average: 16.1%.

Win rate 58.4% — lower than other strategies. Sharpe 1.45. Max drawdown -19.7%. Honestly, looking at just the numbers, it's "fine, I guess."

But here's what's interesting: performance in trending markets is dramatically better. Losses during ranging markets get recovered in a single trending move. Makes sense — institutional accumulation happens when trends are about to start.

## Walk-Forward Validation

All 4 quarters positive, but with high variance. Trending quarters produced great returns, ranging quarters barely survived. This strategy lives on expected value, not consistency.

## Honest Assessment

**The good:**
- Using data that leads price is inherently attractive
- Explosive performance during trending markets
- 4/4 walk-forward quarters (despite the variance)

**The concerns:**
- Binance-only data — misses activity on other exchanges
- Spoofing (fake orders) can deceive the signal
- Ranging markets are a slow bleed

Current status: **Testing** — trending market performance is great, but needs more stability before promotion.
