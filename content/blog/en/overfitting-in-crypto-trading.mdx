# The Overfitting Trap in Crypto Trading

Overfitting is the silent killer of trading strategies. A strategy that achieves 500% returns in backtesting but loses money live is almost certainly overfit. Here's how to spot it and avoid it.

## What is Overfitting?

Overfitting occurs when a model learns the noise in training data rather than the underlying pattern. In trading, this means your strategy has memorized past price movements rather than learning a genuine market inefficiency.

## Warning Signs

1. **Too many parameters** — If your strategy has 10+ tunable parameters, it's likely overfit
2. **Suspiciously high returns** — Monthly returns above 100% should trigger skepticism
3. **Narrow parameter sensitivity** — Slight changes in parameters cause dramatic performance drops
4. **Low trade count** — Fewer trades mean less statistical significance

## How We Fight It

Our AI research pipeline generates ~40 strategies daily. We combat overfitting through:

- **Walk-forward validation** — 4 quarters, 3 must be profitable
- **Realistic execution modeling** — Entry slippage 0.03%, SL slippage 0.05%
- **Paper trading** — 30 days of live market monitoring before deployment
- **Degradation detection** — 70%+ performance drop triggers alerts

## The 3% Survival Rate

Out of ~1,200 strategies tested monthly, only about 3% pass all validation stages. This brutal filter is by design — it's far better to miss a good strategy than to deploy a bad one.
