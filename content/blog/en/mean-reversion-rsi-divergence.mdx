## Discovery

RSI divergence. It's in every trading textbook ever written. Price makes a new low, RSI makes a higher low — selling pressure is weakening. Theoretically perfect.

The question is whether textbook perfection translates to real-world performance. Especially in crypto, where the market runs 24/7 and moves like it had too much coffee. "Do textbook indicators even work here?" I was skeptical. So I tested it.

## The Concept

The basic idea is simple, but this strategy layers on extra filters:

1. **RSI divergence detection**: Price new low + RSI higher low = bullish divergence
2. **Multi-timeframe confirmation**: 5min (entry), 1hr (confirmation), 4hr (trend context)
3. **Volume profile**: Declining volume on the second low = sellers losing interest
4. **Entry/exit**: Enter on confirmed divergence, target the 20-period moving average

The key is the multi-timeframe filter. If the 4hr is trending up and the 5min shows divergence → "pullback bounce in an uptrend" = high probability. If the 4hr is trending down and divergence appears → "bounce attempt during a waterfall" = low probability. This distinction made a huge difference in win rate.

## Backtest Setup

- **Period**: July 2025 — Feb 2026 (8 months)
- **Coins**: BTC, ADA, AVAX
- **Timeframe**: 5min (with 1hr, 4hr confirmation)
- **Fees**: 0.05% per side
- **Execution**: Limit orders

## Results

248 trades, **cumulative return of 156.7%**. Monthly average: 19.6%.

Win rate 65.3%, Sharpe 1.87. Max drawdown -14.5%, which is quite stable. Less volatile than momentum strategies, and impressively, it performs decently even in choppy markets.

## Walk-Forward Validation

All 4 quarters positive. This surprised me. Strategies usually drop at least 1-2 quarters. Mean reversion seems resilient to regime changes — maybe because "price reverts to the mean" is about as close to a universal market law as you can get.

## Honest Assessment

**The good:**
- 4/4 walk-forward quarters positive — that's hard to achieve
- Small max drawdown makes it psychologically easy to stick with
- Works in choppy markets (compensates for momentum strategies' weakness)

**The concerns:**
- Absolute returns are lower than momentum strategies (stability/return tradeoff)
- In a strong downtrend, you can get a string of failed bottom-picks
- Multi-timeframe confirmation means entries are sometimes late
