## Discovery

"Crypto trades 24/7, where would gaps come from?" — that was my first reaction. But looking at the data, there's a genuine price dislocation between Friday night (when institutions leave) and Monday morning (when Asian desks come online). Weekend liquidity is thin, so prices drift.

Found an analysis of this pattern during a web search. 65-70% of weekend gaps partially fill on Monday, across 3 years of data. The numbers looked plausible. Time to run it.

## The Concept

1. **Gap measurement**: Price at Friday 21:00 UTC (US desk close) vs Monday 00:00 UTC (Asia desk open)
2. **Minimum threshold**: Only trade gaps above 0.5% for BTC, 1% for altcoins
3. **Direction**: If price went up over the weekend → short (expect fill down). Down → long (fill up)
4. **Target**: 50-70% gap fill (not 100% — full fills are unreliable)
5. **Time limit**: Close after 12 hours if the gap hasn't filled

## Backtest Setup

- **Period**: July 2025 — Feb 2026 (8 months)
- **Coins**: BTC, ETH
- **Historical data**: Based on 3 years (2023-2025) of weekend gap statistics
- **Fees**: 0.05% per side

## Results

94 trades (only one per week, so few), **cumulative return of 67.2%**. Monthly average: 8.4%.

Win rate 71.2%, Sharpe 1.12, max drawdown -12.3%. Numbers look decent on the surface. But...

## Walk-Forward Validation

Here's where it gets ugly. Performance is **declining** quarter by quarter. Early quarters were strong, but the most recent quarter produced a small loss. The edge is disappearing.

## Honest Assessment

**The good:**
- Clear, intuitive logic
- High win rate
- Once per week = easy to manage

**The concerns:**
- The edge is clearly shrinking — more people know about this pattern, or weekend institutional activity is increasing
- One trade per week makes it hard to reach statistical significance
- In strong trends, gaps in the trend direction don't fill

Current status: **Degraded** — the historical edge was real, but market microstructure changes are eroding the pattern. Honestly, this strategy is dying.
