## Discovery

Found a "Multi-Timeframe Alignment" concept while digging through open-source trading libraries. The idea is dead simple: looking at one timeframe gets you faked out constantly, so **only enter when all three timeframes agree** on direction.

"Everyone must agree" is like a unanimous vote requirement. Seems inefficient, but if the signals that pass are genuinely higher quality? Maybe a senate is better than a democracy. (Historically dubious, but in trading...)

## The Concept

Each timeframe answers a different question:

**4-hour (Strategic)**: "What's the dominant trend?"
- 50-period EMA slope + ADX
- Only trade in this direction. If 4hr says down, no longs regardless of lower timeframe signals.

**1-hour (Tactical)**: "Is the trend currently active?"
- MACD crossover for confirmation
- Even if the 4hr trend exists, if price is consolidating or pulling back, skip.

**5-minute (Execution)**: "Where do I enter?"
- 8/21 EMA crossover for precise timing
- Only triggers when aligned with both higher timeframes.

Statistically, each filter independently removes ~50% of signals. Three filters in series means roughly 1 in 8 potential signals survive. The survivors have significantly higher probability of being genuine trend moves.

## Backtest Setup

- **Period**: Sep 2025 — Feb 2026 (6 months)
- **Coins**: SOL, LINK, AVAX, SUI, BCH
- **Exit**: Trailing stop (2x ATR, tightens as profit grows)
- **Timeout**: Close if no profit after 4 hours (48 candles on 5min)

## Results

203 trades, **cumulative return of 94.5%**. Monthly average: 15.8%.

Win rate is only 55.2%. But average winners are 1.8-2.2x average losers. Classic trend-following profile — lose small, win big.

Max drawdown -25.4% is concerning. The worst drawdown happened during a trend-to-range transition, when the strategy kept expecting continuation that never came.

## Walk-Forward Validation

3 out of 4 quarters positive. The losing quarter was during extended sideways consolidation. When trends are alive, this strategy delivers. When they're not, it's dead weight.

## Honest Assessment

**The good:**
- Triple filter genuinely eliminates false signals
- Excellent in trending markets
- Different coins trend at different times → natural rotation

**The concerns:**
- 25.4% max drawdown is uncomfortable
- Trend-to-range transitions cause consecutive losses
- The 4hr filter is slow — can keep the strategy in "no trade" mode for too long
- 6 months of backtest data is on the short side

Current status: **Testing** — trending market performance is impressive, but drawdown management and regime transitions need work.
